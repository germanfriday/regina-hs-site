Congratulations Coach Laffey!!!

We congratulate Varsity Softball Coach Diane Laffey on her 1000th Softball win!! The win came on Saturday, May 1, 2010 during the Michigan Stars Tournament at Liberty Park in Sterling Heights in a no hitter against Alpena.

Ms. Laffey is the first coach in MHSAA history to reach the 1000 win mark.  In her 40 years as softball coach at Regina, Ms. Laffey has a .744 winning percentage and 5 State Softball Championships!

We're so proud she's a Saddlelite!

Regina Alum Nikki Nemitz is named Pitcher of the Year at U of M

Nemitz named U-M's top pitcher Regina graduate posted 24-5 record for the Wolverines

Wednesday, May 13, 2009

The Macomb Daily Sports Staff

Michigan junior pitcher Nikki Nemitz, a graduate of Regina, was named the softball team's Most Outstanding Pitcher at an awards banquet May 11.

Nemitz helped the Wolverines earn the fifth seed in the upcoming NCAA Tournament by going 24-5 with a Big Ten-best 0.90 earned-run average.

She also struck out 277 batters and threw five shutouts.

The Varsity Soccer team defeated Lake Shore High School 1-0, Saturday June 7th, to win a District Championship. Regina 's Senior Class of 2008 was the most successful soccer class in school history. Meet (2007)

Regina High School runners Mallory Sneden, Anna Maria Paruk, Beth Ponkowski and Gina Valgoi placed sixth in the 4 x 800 relay at the Division II state track championship on June 2, 2007 at Zeeland High School with a school record time of 9:48.49. Gina Valgoi is also a two-time All-Stater in cross country and holds the CYO mile record. The Regina team is coached by Gregg Golden, Becky Hendren and Bob Valgoi.
