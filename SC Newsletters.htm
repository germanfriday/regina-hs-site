SPOrTS Club News

On behalf of the Regina High School Sports Club, I would like to thank everyone who sacrificed their time and energy in support of the Lemon Chill.  This annual fundraiser is a huge project for us.  Without the participation of the students, parents and friends of Regina High school, we could not have done it. We netted over $18,000 for our athletic program.  Thank you to the Davis family of Prairie Farms Dairy for their faith and support.  We'll see you all next year.

3-5 Year Development Plan -

The SPOrTS Club has formed a sub-committee to establish a 3 to 5 year plan for athletic facilities at Regina's new location.  The committee was established to provide a planning group for how best to develop available space for use by Regina's athletic teams.  The kick-off meeting was held Wednesday, October 11, 2006 with a second meeting on October 24.  The committed set goals, objectives, and action items, and is proceeding to develop a plan.  The goal is to have a prioritized development schedule by the end of the year.

Laffey Inducted -

October 30, 2006 was another momentous moment for Regina's Diane Laffey as she was inducted into the Michigan Sports Hall of Fame.  Diane is ranked first in the state for career softball victories (907) and fourth for career girls basketball victories (539).  Present were members of the Regina school staff, her close friends, members of the Regina SPOrTS Club, and past graduates.  Diane provided an acceptance speech that set the tone for the entire evening.  She credited her success to being blessed with, and surrounded by great talent and support on and off the field and court.  Host Frank Beckman introduced Diane to lead off the program which included inductees Jimmy Devellano, Dave Diles, Sonny Grandelius, Greg Kelser, Dan Majerle, Jack McCloskey, and Frank Tanana.  Diane's record definitely puts her in position to lead that all-star cast. Well deserved Diane.
